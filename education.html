High School Education

A transformative journey from Form 1 to Form 4, blending academic rigour with character development

Secondary Education at Paul Boit

Our High School in Turbo, Uasin Gishu County, offers a holistic, CBC-aligned education for young men aged 14 to 18. We cater to Form 1–4 students with a comprehensive programme that builds both academic excellence and moral grounding.

In Forms 1 and 2, learners follow a broad CBC curriculum that prepares them for pathway selection. In Forms 3 and 4, students specialise in one of three pathways: STEM, Social Sciences, or Arts & Sports. Our graduates transition to universities and colleges with strong KCSE results and life skills.

CBC Pathways & Curriculum

Our curriculum is anchored on the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC), designed to nurture competent and engaged learners. In Forms 1–2, students explore a broad range of subjects including English, Kiswahili, Mathematics, Integrated Science, Social Studies, CRE, Business Studies, Agriculture, and Sports.

From Form 3, learners choose one of three pathways:

  • STEM: Advanced sciences, ICT, engineering, and mathematics.
  • Social Sciences: Humanities, business, and languages.
  • Arts & Sports: Music, drama, fine art, and physical education.

Each pathway culminates in KCSE examinations, with our students consistently achieving mean scores above the national average.

Entry Requirements

We welcome students from various educational backgrounds. Typically, new students join us in Form 1 after sitting for KCPE. Admission is granted on a case-by-case basis, subject to:

  • KCPE aggregate of 280 marks and above
  • Satisfactory performance in entrance interviews
  • Character assessment from previous school

For Form 3 entry, students must demonstrate capability in their chosen pathway. Transfers from other schools are handled individually with the principal.

Life Beyond Classrooms

Our students thrive through a rich extracurricular programme that includes sports, arts, and leadership opportunities. Weekly fixtures and tournaments build camaraderie and resilience.

Sports

  • Rugby (regional champions)
  • Soccer
  • Volleyball
  • Athletics

The Arts

  • Drama festivals
  • Choir & GLEE Club
  • Fine art exhibitions
  • Music concerts

Student leadership – from prefectship to club leadership – is encouraged to foster responsibility and organisational skills, preparing our young men for university and beyond.

Boarding & Student Support

As a fully boarding school, we provide a safe, nurturing environment where students live and learn together. Our dormitories are supervised by trained houseparents, and we offer:

  • Nutritious meals prepared on-site
  • Transport for weekend travel (arranged with parents)
  • 24/7 health centre with nurse
  • Evening preps with teacher supervision
