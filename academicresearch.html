Academic Research

Advancing knowledge through inquiry, innovation, and collaboration.

Research at Paul Boit Boys

At Paul Boit Boys, we believe that research is not just for universities – it begins in high school. Our students and teachers engage in cutting-edge projects across STEM, social sciences, and the arts, often in partnership with local universities and research institutions.

From developing low-cost water purification systems to publishing papers on Kenyan history, our academic research culture fosters curiosity, critical thinking, and real-world problem-solving. Many of our alumni have gone on to pursue advanced degrees and research careers.

Current Research Areas

Renewable Energy

Students are designing solar-powered water heaters and biogas digesters for local communities.

Environmental Conservation

Research on tree species restoration and water catchment protection in Turbo region.

Robotics & AI

Building automated systems for sorting and monitoring – national science congress winners.

Oral Histories

Documenting the stories of local elders and preserving Kalenjin traditions.

Social Impact

Surveys on youth employment and education access in Uasin Gishu County.

Health Science

Partnership with local hospitals to study nutrition and disease prevention.

Spotlight: Solar Water Heater

A team of Form 4 students designed and built a low-cost solar water heater using recycled materials. The prototype now provides hot water for the school kitchen, saving firewood and reducing costs. The project won first prize at the Regional Science and Engineering Fair and is being scaled for community use.

Student researchers: Brian Kiprop, Evans Rotich, Mark Kipchumba
Mentor: Mr. Otieno (Physics)
