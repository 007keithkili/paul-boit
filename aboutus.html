About Paul Boit Boys

Discover our rich history, guiding principles, and the dedicated community that shapes young men of character.

Our Story

Founded in 1985, Paul Boit Boys High School began as a modest day school with just 80 students and a vision to provide quality education to the boys of Turbo Sub-county. Named after the renowned Kenyan athlete Paul Boit, the school was built on principles of discipline, hard work, and community.

Over four decades, we have grown into a premier boys' boarding institution with over 1,300 students. Our alumni include doctors, engineers, business leaders, and sportsmen who continue to make Kenya proud. Despite our growth, we remain committed to the values that defined our early years: integrity, resilience, and excellence.

Our Mission

To nurture disciplined, responsible, and academically excellent young men through quality education, mentorship, and character development in a supportive Christian environment.

Our Vision

To be a center of academic and co-curricular excellence producing holistic leaders who positively impact society, guided by strong moral values.

Our Core Values

Integrity

We uphold honesty and strong moral principles in all we do.

Resilience

We encourage perseverance and determination in the face of challenges.

Excellence

We strive for the highest standards in academics and character.

Teamwork

We work together, supporting one another as a school community.

From the Principal's Desk

Mr. John Kimutai

"At Paul Boit Boys, we believe that education is more than passing exams. It is about shaping young men of character – gentlemen who will lead with integrity, serve their communities, and make a difference in the world."

— Principal, Paul Boit Boys High School

With over 20 years of experience in education, Mr. Kimutai leads a dedicated team of teachers and staff committed to nurturing each student's potential. Under his leadership, the school has achieved record KCSE results and expanded its co-curricular programmes.
